The Challenge

The Peacock Intrusion Problem

Farmers are currently facing severe challenges with crop damage caused by peafowl incursions. This not only threatens agricultural livelihoods but also disrupts the delicate balance between community sustenance and wildlife conservation.

  • Crop Devastation

    Significant economic losses due to foraging and trampling of sensitive crops.

  • Legal & Ethical Constraints

    Peacocks are protected wildlife. Deterrence must be absolutely humane and legally compliant.

The Old Way Fails

Traditional netting and scarecrows are ineffective long term and can sometimes pose hazards to the birds themselves.

We Need Technology

We require a humane, legal, and technology-best solution that deters without harm. An intelligent approach to coexistence.

Our Approach

Design & Develop

Overview of the proposed project solution and our strategic task distribution approach.

Acoustic Deterrence

Utilizing specific, harmless sound frequencies proven to deter peafowl naturally without causing auditory damage or distress to other domestic animals.

Smart Automation

Intelligent sensors that detect intrusion patterns and dynamically adjust sound emissions, ensuring long-term effectiveness by preventing habituation.

Task Distribution

A structured approach dividing responsibilities across hardware prototyping, software algorithms, field deployment, and ecological monitoring.

Execution Strategy

Phases of Development

A clear roadmap from initial concept to full-scale AI integration.

1

Low Cost MVP

Developing the Minimum Viable Product. A cost-effective, basic hardware setup generating predetermined sound waves to establish initial proof-of-concept.

2

Manufacturing & Testing

Scaling up the robust design for real-field testing. Deploying units across multiple agricultural zones to gather empirical data on deterrence stamina and weather durability.

3

AI Integration

Full automation phase. Implementing machine learning models to visually/acoustically identify peafowl specifically, triggering the deterrence system only when necessary.
